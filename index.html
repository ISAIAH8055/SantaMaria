History of Santa Maria

Santa Maria, established in the early 1800s, has grown into a bustling city known for its agricultural roots, particularly in the fields of strawberries and wine production. The city's location in the Central Coast of California has contributed to its success as a regional hub of commerce.

Over time, Santa Maria has expanded from a small settlement to a city with a rich cultural heritage and a diverse population. The city is also known for its iconic BBQ style, Santa Maria-style tri-tip, and its annual festivals.
